# MoyaCode — Objection Handling Quick Reference

Rule: never argue with an objection. Acknowledge → redirect to value → move to close.

## Price / Cost

- **"It's expensive."**
  → "I understand. Can I ask — what would it mean if your child could build their own app or website next year?"
  (Reframe cost as investment. Don't discount on the doorstep unless authorized.)

- **"I can't afford it right now."**
  → "No pressure at all. Can I add you to our WhatsApp so you have the details for when it works for you?" (Keep the door open — capture contact, don't lose the lead.)

## Relevance / Necessity

- **"Is this even necessary? My child is doing fine in school."**
  → "Totally understandable. This isn't a replacement for school — it's an extra skill, like a foreign language. The kids who start early are the ones who'll have the advantage later."

- **"We already do computer studies at school."**
  → (For schools/principals) "That's great groundwork. MoyaCode goes further — actual coding, AI, and robotics projects, not just computer literacy. It complements what you're already doing."

## Logistics

- **"We don't have a computer at home."**
  → "No problem — it works on a phone too." *(confirm this is accurate before the campaign goes live — if not yet phone-optimized, say instead: "Let's talk about how you could access one," and note it in `market-research/insights-log.md` as a product gap.)*

- **"My child is too young / too old."**
  → "MoyaCode covers JSS1 all the way to SS3, so there's a place for them."

- **"We're very busy, no time for extra classes."**
  → "It's self-paced — your child can learn at their own time, no fixed timetable to clash with."

## Trust / Unfamiliarity

- **"I've never heard of MoyaCode."**
  → Show the video/flyer immediately. Visual proof beats explanation. Mention it's already running with other families/schools if true.

- **"Is this some scam / online thing?"**
  → Give the concrete, tangible details up front: WhatsApp number, website, and (if applicable) a real local contact/address. Avoid vague online-only language.

## Stalling

- **"Let me think about it."**
  → "Of course, take your time. Can I add you to our WhatsApp so you have the details whenever you're ready?" (Always capture contact before leaving — see `sales-script-door-to-door.md` Step 5.)

---

**Golden rule across all objections:** the goal of the doorstep conversation is never to win the argument — it's to leave with a contact and a scheduled follow-up. If an objection can't be resolved on the spot, don't force it; bank the contact and let the follow-up (video, testimonial, second conversation) do the work.
