# MoyaCode — Ideal Customer Profiles (ICP)

Three distinct audiences. Each needs a different opening line and a different proof point. Never pitch all three with the same words.

## 1. The Parent (the payer)

- **Who:** Parents/guardians of children in JSS1–SS3 in Owo and environs.
- **What they actually care about:** their child's future, not falling behind peers, practical skills over "just playing games on the phone."
- **Emotional driver:** **loss aversion beats gain-seeking.** "Is my child being left behind?" moves faster than "here's a fun opportunity."
- **Proof that works:** the video, a real example of what a student built, word-of-mouth from another parent.
- **Common hesitations:** cost, "is this necessary," no computer at home, unfamiliarity with AI/coding as a subject.

## 2. The Child (the user)

- **Who:** The student who will actually sit through the lessons.
- **What they care about:** fun, status among friends, "is this cool," badges/achievement, MoyaBot.
- **Emotional driver:** excitement and identity ("I'm a builder, not just a phone user").
- **Proof that works:** MoyaBot mascot, gamified XP/badges, the energetic parts of the video.
- **Role in the sale:** children influence parents more than we give them credit for — if the child is excited in front of the parent, the sale gets easier. Let kids watch the video too, not just parents.

## 3. The School / Principal (the gatekeeper)

- **Who:** Head teachers, principals, proprietors of schools in Owo.
- **What they care about:** their school's reputation, results, zero extra burden on staff/timetable.
- **Emotional driver:** credibility and low effort — "does this make my school look good, and do I have to do any work?"
- **Proof that works:** structured curriculum (JSS1–SS3), self-paced (no timetable disruption), clean professional flyer, an offer to run a free demo session.
- **Common hesitations:** "we already have computer studies," liability/safety, scheduling.

---

## Universal buying triggers to lean on across all three

1. **Loss aversion** — "being left behind" is a stronger motivator than "getting ahead."
2. **Social proof** — other parents/schools already doing this in the area.
3. **Low effort / self-paced** — removes the biggest objection (time, timetable, no computer required if phone-friendly).
4. **Visual proof over verbal claims** — always show the video/flyer, don't just describe it.
