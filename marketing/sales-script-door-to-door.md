# MoyaCode — Door-to-Door / Community Sales Script

**Goal of every visit:** get a WhatsApp contact or a follow-up commitment. Do NOT try to close on the spot — the sale happens in the follow-up, not the first knock.

**Before you go:** have the video and flyer saved to your phone (so they work with no signal). Have a notebook or your phone's notes app ready to log every name.

---

## Step 1 — Opening (10–15 seconds)

> "Good afternoon ma/sir, my name is ___. I'm not selling anything today — I just want two minutes of your time about something for your child's future. Is that okay?"

Small, low-pressure ask lowers resistance immediately. Smile, be brief, don't rush into the pitch.

## Step 2 — Ask, don't pitch (let them talk)

Ask **one** of these, then go quiet and actually listen:

- "Does your child use a phone or computer at home?"
- "Have you thought about what skills your child will need in future, with all this AI now?"
- "Is your child in JSS or SS now?"

Listen for **worry** vs **excitement** vs **indifference** — this decides your next line.

## Step 3 — Tailored pitch (30–45 seconds, then show the video)

**If they showed worry** (most common — lead with this instinct):
> "That's exactly why I'm here. Right now in Owo, we're running MoyaCode — kids learn coding, AI, and robotics. Not just using phones, but building with them. Many children are already ahead, and this is how your child can be part of that."

**If they showed excitement:**
> "That's great! MoyaCode is exactly for that. Kids learn coding, AI and robotics, step by step, no experience needed — from JSS1 to SS3."

Then **show the 30-second video** on your phone. Let it do the emotional work — don't talk over it.

## Step 4 — Handle objections (only if raised)

| Objection | Response |
|---|---|
| "It's expensive" | "I understand. Can I ask — what would it mean if your child could build their own app or website next year?" (redirect to value, don't argue price) |
| "We don't have a computer" | "No problem — it works on a phone too." |
| "My child is too young / too old" | "MoyaCode covers JSS1 all the way to SS3, so there's a place for them." |
| "Let me think about it" | "Of course, no pressure. Can I add you to our WhatsApp so you have the details whenever you're ready?" |

Never argue. Redirect to value, then move to the close.

## Step 5 — Close (always leave with ONE of these, in order of preference)

1. "Can I add your number to our WhatsApp so I can send you the details?" — **best outcome**
2. "Can I note your child's name and class so I follow up with you?"
3. At minimum: "Please take this flyer — my number is on it."

**Never leave a doorstep with nothing captured.**

## Step 6 — Referral ask (do this even after a "maybe")

> "Thank you! Do you know any other parent nearby who might want their child to learn this too?"

---

## The Follow-Up Rule (this is where the real sale happens)

Every contact gets a WhatsApp message **within 24 hours.** Most parents say "not now" on the first visit not because they're uninterested, but because they're not ready yet. Reps who give up after one knock leave the sale on the table.

**Suggested follow-up (send 3–5 days after first contact, not immediately):**
> "Hi [Name], it's [You] from MoyaCode 👋 Just wanted to share — here's what one of our students built this week 🤖 [video/photo]. Let me know if you'd like [child]'s name added to this term's list."
