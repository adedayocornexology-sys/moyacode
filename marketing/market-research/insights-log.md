## 2026-07-01 — Seed research: pricing benchmarks & small-town marketing channels

**Key Findings**

- **Pricing benchmarks:** General Nigerian coding bootcamps range from ~₦200,000 to
  ₦2,000,000 (avg. ~$2,500), often with payment plans. Kids-specific programs (e.g. BiZmarrow's
  Kids Coding Bootcamp) run shorter formats — 2 hours/day, Mon–Fri — rather than full bootcamp
  pricing. WAAW Foundation explicitly markets itself as more "pocket-friendly" than standard
  bootcamps as a competitive angle. **Takeaway: affordability positioning is an active,
  recognized differentiator in this market, not just a nice-to-have.**
  [Nucamp — Top 5 Coding Bootcamps in Nigeria](https://www.nucamp.co/blog/coding-bootcamp-nigeria-nga-top-5-best-coding-bootcamps-in-nigeria-in-2025) ·
  [BiZmarrow Kids Coding Bootcamp](https://www.bizmarrow.com/kids-coding-camp-for-future-tech-leaders-in-nigeria/) ·
  [Code Campus International](https://codecampus.com.ng/)

- **What actually moves small-town Nigerian parents (ranked by perceived effectiveness in
  published research):** 1) **Quality of the programme itself** (word of mouth on results),
  2) infrastructure/visible credibility, 3) **TV/Radio adverts** — ranked *above* social media
  in these contexts, 4) **face-to-face conversation with parents.** Social media/website matters
  but is not the top-ranked channel outside major cities.
  [Truehost — How to Market a School in Nigeria](https://truehost.com.ng/how-to-market-a-school-in-nigeria/) ·
  [Marketing Strategies and Enrolment, Cross River State study (PDF)](https://files.eric.ed.gov/fulltext/ED560683.pdf)

- **Testimonials compound:** encouraging satisfied parents to share experiences (even informally,
  word-of-mouth or a WhatsApp status repost) was highlighted as a credibility driver equal to
  formal advertising in these studies.

**Recommendations for MoyaCode**

1. **Lead with affordability/accessibility in Owo messaging** — since it's already a proven
   differentiator regionally, and Owo is a smaller/lower-income-density market than Lagos.
   Confirm actual pricing before this becomes a claim in the flyer/video.
2. **Don't neglect low-tech channels.** Door-to-door (already in motion) and a short local
   radio spot (if an Owo/Ondo community radio station is affordable) may outperform additional
   social media spend at this stage — face-to-face and radio both ranked above social in the
   research above.
3. **Systematize testimonials early.** After the first cohort, capture 2–3 short parent/child
   video testimonials — this becomes the single highest-leverage asset for the next enrollment
   wave, per both the door-to-door script's "show, don't tell" principle and this research.
4. **Keep the "no experience needed / self-paced" framing** — it directly counters the
   "quality of programme" and "low effort" concerns that rank highest with this audience.
